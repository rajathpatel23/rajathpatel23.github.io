I am a Machine Learning Engineer at Interos Inc. where I work with Information Extraction team building machine learning models to extract information from highly unstructured data sources. My work focuses specifically on building scalable NLP based models to solve problems like entity linking, entity extraction, and event detection. I have also worked on a computer vision problem for building a scalable search engine with an active learning component.

Prior to joining Interos Inc., I was a graduate student at University of Maryland Baltimore County (UMBC) , where I was advised by Dr.Francis Ferraro. My research was focused on building multi-task learning model to understand the complementary of different task like knowledge graph embedding, fine-grain entity type prediction, language model etc by learning them through shared representation.

During my time at UMBC, I also worked under Dr. Shiming Yang at the University of Maryland, School of Medicine (UMed) on exploring deep learning techniques to detect massive transfusion in trauma patients by studying their photoplethysmogram (PPG) signal My work at UMed specifically involved signal processing, representation learning and dimensionality reductions for processing PPG signals to study the underlying pattern through deep learning.
